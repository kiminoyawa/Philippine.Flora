PHILIPPINE FLORA

10 FAMOUS PHILIPPINE LOCAL PLANTS

SAMPAGUITA

Jasminum sambac commonly known as Sampaguita was declared as the national flower by Governor-General Frank Murphy in 1934. Its white flower is believed to symbolize purity, fidelity, and hope.

JADE VINE

Strongylodon macrobotrys, commonly known as jade vine, emerald vine or turquoise jade vine, is a species of leguminous perennial liana (woody vine) endemic to the tropical forests of the Philippines.

NARRA

Pterocarpus indicus is a large deciduous reddish hardwood tree that is native to most Asian countries. Widely introduced due to its many uses, the narra was declared the national tree of the Philippines in 1934.

RAFFLESIA

Rafflesia philippensis is a parasitic plant species of the Rafflesiaceae family that was named by Francisco Manuel Blanco in his Flora de Filipinas in 1845. The species is known only from the Philippines.

PALAWAN CHERRY BLOSSOM

Balayong and Cassia nodosa, is a tropical flowering tree native to the Palawan province in the Philippines, but it's not a cherry tree at all.

WALING WALING

Vanda sanderiana is a species of orchid. It is commonly called Waling-waling in the Philippines and is also called Sander's Vanda, after Henry Frederick Conrad Sander.

FOXTAIL ORCHID

Showy Medinilla (Medinilla magnifica) is actually an epiphyte, a plant that grows on other trees but does not withdraw its food from those trees as parasites do.

WATER LILIES

Water hyacinth has severely infested Philippine waters with its rapid rate of proliferation is the most damaging aquatic plant worldwide because of its capability to reproduce.

EASTER LILY

The large white trumpet-shaped flowers of Easter lily, Lilium longiflorum, are a tradition at this time of year.



QUEEN OF PHILIPPINE

Philippine mussaenda (Mussaenda philippica) Flower, Leaf, Care, Uses - PictureThis. Philippine mussaenda (Mussaenda philippica) lives up to its nickname, Queen of the Philippines. Growing up to 10 m tall, and covered in draping pink or white blooms.

IMPORTANCE OF LEARNING THIS

It is crucial to know about the local plants in our country in order to protect them. Throughout history, plants have been essential to the survival of both people and animals. Our food, animal feed, clothing, and the active elements in our medications are all derived from plants. Furthermore, living plants are necessary for the biosphere—the living environment that humans live in—to function properly. Carbon dioxide and oxygen are produced by plants from the atmosphere. Preserving our nation's heritage can also be aided by maintaining the indigenous flora.
